Understanding “Synonyms”


Using different words that mean the same thing makes our talking and writing more fun. It's like painting with lots of colours instead of just one!

Remember, you can always swap a word for its buddy:

  • A "fun day" can be a "great day"
  • A "loud noise" can be a "noisy sound"
  • A "smart kid" can be a "clever child"

Words that I learnt from the story


Hugged:Held someone closely in your arms.
Sentence: She hugged her teddy bear before going to bed.

Sandbox: A box or area filled with sand where children can play.
Sentence: The kids made a sandcastle in the sandbox.

Castle: A large, strong building where kings and queens used to live.
Sentence: They toured the old castle on their vacation.
